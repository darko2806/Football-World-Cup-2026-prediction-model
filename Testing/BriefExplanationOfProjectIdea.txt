WORLD CUP 2026 MODEL
The world cup has 12 groups of 4 teams, 48 teams in total. All top two advance to the next leg of the tournament and the 8 best third-placed teams also.

MAIN OBJECTIVE: 
Create a model that predicts the expected goals for each team in a certain match (model has two outputs for two different teams)
                                ||
                                ||
                                ||
Use those predicted expected goals values as lambda values for number of goals for the first team and the second team    
                                ||
                                ||
                                ||
Using Poisson distribution and the lambda values, we get the probabilities by implemeting this logic:

Team 1 win probability = sum of P(G1 = i, G2 = j) over all i > j

Draw probability = sum of P(G1 = i, G2 = j) over all i = j

Team 2 win probability = sum of P(G1 = i, G2 = j) over all i < j

We should definitely run a large number of simulations to get more precise results (n_simulations => large number around 100000)
